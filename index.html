"Mind over matter,
but soul over all."

"If I can re-learn how to ride a board without bindings, I can learn to ride one with bindings"

10 days before Evan's 18th birthday his left leg was amputated. Evan had just signed his first skateboard sponsorship deal.

At his birthday party in the hospital, he swore he'd ride a skateboard again. The snowboard followed naturally.


Evan made history winning

GOLD

in Men's Snowboard Cross at the
Sochi Paralympics (2014),
the first to include
snowboarding events,
and

SILVER

in Men's Banked Slalom at the
PyeongChang Paralympics (2018)

“When your brain starts saying
‘I can’t do this’
you’re only about 40%
into your capacity.”

Evan cross-trains with kettlebells, tight ropes, bouldering, and skateboarding for peak core strength, agility, and balance.

"I like being the guy with the target on his back...The one to beat."

What more can a Paralympian accomplish? The Olympics.

The 30-year-old Strong hopes become the first Paralympic snowboarder to compete against able-bodied athletes in the Winter Olympics.

Evan's request is being considered by the Olympic Committee. If approved and his race record supports inclusion, he would compete in able-bodied events at the 2022 Beijing Games.
